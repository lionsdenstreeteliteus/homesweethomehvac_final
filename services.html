Ventilation

Ventilation services involve testing, maintaining, and calibrating systems that exchange stale indoor air for fresh outdoor air to improve indoor air quality by removing pollutants, moisture, and odors. These services ensure ventilation systems, which can be natural (like open windows), mechanical (like fans and ductwork), or a combination, operate efficiently to provide clean, healthy air and prevent the buildup of contaminants. Purpose of Ventilation Services

Home Sweet Home Offer All these Services:
  • Maintain Indoor Air Quality (IAQ): Services ensure the removal of harmful airborne pollutants, dust, smoke, carbon dioxide, and moisture, which can negatively impact health.
  • Ensure Occupant Health & Safety: By providing fresh, oxygen-rich air and removing harmful elements, ventilation services contribute to a healthy and safe environment.
  • Regulate Temperature and Humidity: Proper ventilation helps to control the temperature and moisture levels within a space, contributing to thermal comfort.
  • Optimize System Efficiency: Services check for proper airflow, balance, and calibration to confirm the system functions correctly and efficiently, which also helps prevent energy waste.
Types of Ventilation
  • Natural Ventilation: Uses natural forces like wind and pressure differences to exchange air through openings like windows and doors.
  • Mechanical Ventilation: Employs fans, ducts, and other equipment to actively draw in fresh air and exhaust stale air, ensuring consistent air exchange.
  • Mixed-Mode Ventilation: Combines natural and mechanical strategies to take advantage of both approaches.
What's Involved in a Ventilation Service
  • Inspection and Testing: A trained professional checks the system for issues such as air leaks, dirt buildup, or improper airflow.
  • Balancing and Calibration: The system is adjusted to ensure the right amount of fresh air is delivered and stale air is removed effectively.
  • Duct Cleaning: This process uses specialized equipment to remove dust and debris from the ductwork, improving airflow and air quality.
  • Repair and Maintenance: Service engineers identify and fix problems to ensure the system operates as intended and prevent potential health or comfort issues.

Water Heater Repair & Install Services

Just like your plumbing is a workhorse, so is your water heater. This appliance works almost constantly. Not only does every faucet in your house depend on the water heater, but so do appliances such as your washing machine and dishwasher.
Like any hardworking appliance, the water heater is subject to a variety of maintenance issues and common problems. If you’re having issues with your water heater, call our team of local plumbers by phone or schedule an appointment online to receive assistance. Snell Heating & Air Conditioning provides plumbing services to residents in Virginia.
One of the most common complaints about water heaters is an insufficient hot water supply. How could this be? There are a multitude of reasons behind this:

Leaks: Water leaks can be caused by a faulty temperature and pressure (T&P) relief valve, excessive pressure, overheating, a stuck valve, a leak from an overhead or adjacent plumbing connection, loose heating element bolts, a bad gasket or a leaking water tank.
Incoming Water Too Cold: In cold-climate areas, the incoming water supply can be very, very cold in the wintertime, which means your water heater will require considerably more time to heat the water. As a result, you may feel that you’re not getting the same volume of hot water as before.
Faulty Thermostat: If you notice no increase in water temperature when you increase the setting on your water heater, it’s possible that the thermostat is faulty. This is fairly common with electric water heaters, which have thermostats attached to both the upper and lower heating elements on the tank.
